Understanding Urban Shade

The Challenge

As cities become increasingly dense and climate change intensifies urban heat, shade has become a crucial factor in pedestrian comfort and public health. Amsterdam's urban infrastructure varies dramatically in its ability to provide shade throughout the day.

Our Approach

SlimShady analyzes shade availability across Amsterdam's sidewalks, roads, and cycle lanes using high-resolution spatial data and shadow modeling. We calculate shade availability indices that account for varying sun positions throughout the day, providing a comprehensive view of urban comfort zones.

How to Use the Map

Overview Mode: Neighborhoods are colored by their average shade quality. Click any area to dive deeper into the analysis.

Infrastructure Layers: Toggle between sidewalks, roads, and cycle lanes using the layer control. Statistics focus on sidewalk data for pedestrian comfort.

Detailed Analysis: Click on neighborhoods to see individual segment analysis, statistical summaries, and shade distribution metrics.

Interpreting Results

Shade indices range from 0.0 (no shade) to 1.0 (complete shade). Our categories help interpret livability: Poor (0-0.5), Acceptable (0.5-0.7), Very Good (0.7-0.9), and Excellent (0.9-1.0) shading.
